FAQs

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between the soft cap and the hard cap?

The soft cap is the minimum amount a presale needs to raise for the project to proceed; if it isn't reached, contributions are typically refunded. The hard cap is the maximum amount the presale will accept — once it's hit, the round closes even if demand is higher.

How is the token price set during a presale?

Most presales sell tokens in sequential stages at rising prices (seed, private, public, final), rewarding earlier participants with a lower entry price. The final stage price is usually set below the target exchange listing price.

What does the risk rating actually measure?

It's a model-based score built from public, checkable signals — audit status, liquidity lock terms, contract verification and team KYC. It does not evaluate the team's execution ability, market demand for the product, or token price performance after listing.

Are presale tokens locked after purchase?

Often, yes. Many presales apply a vesting schedule to public contributors as well as the team, releasing tokens gradually after listing rather than all at once. Always check the specific project's vesting terms before contributing.

Is participating in a crypto presale safe?

No presale is "safe" in the way a bank deposit is. Presale tokens are early-stage, illiquid, and carry a real risk of total loss — from failed launches, rug pulls, or the token simply never trading above its presale price.
